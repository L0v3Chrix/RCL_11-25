# Stakeholder Interview Question Templates

**Date:** October 21, 2025
**Project:** Recovery Centered Living Website Redesign
**Purpose:** Extract insights for content creation, feature prioritization, and messaging refinement

---

## Interview Structure Guidelines

### Pre-Interview Preparation
- **Schedule:** 45-60 minutes per stakeholder
- **Format:** Video call (record with permission) or in-person
- **Documentation:** Take detailed notes, record direct quotes
- **Follow-up:** Send summary for validation within 24 hours

### Interview Approach
- **Start with context:** Explain website goals and their role
- **Active listening:** Let them elaborate beyond yes/no answers
- **Follow curiosity:** Ask "tell me more about that" when interesting points emerge
- **Capture emotion:** Note tone, energy, and passion around topics
- **Visual aids:** Show competitor examples, wireframes for reaction

## 1. Interview Questions for Slade Skaggs (Founder/CEO)

**Context:** Slade's transformation story is the heart of RCL's authenticity. These questions capture his vision, values, and what makes RCL different.

### Personal Journey & Vision (15 minutes)

1. **Walk me through the moment you decided to create Recovery Centered Living.** What was the specific spark?

2. **In your 25 years of struggle, what did you need that didn't exist?** What gap were you determined to fill?

3. **What does "recovery centered" mean to you personally?** How is that different from other approaches you experienced?

4. **When you look at someone just walking through the door, what do you see that others might miss?** What do you know they're feeling?

5. **Tell me about a moment in the past year where you thought "THIS is why we exist."** What happened?

6. **If you could only communicate ONE thing to families searching for help right now, what would it be?**

7. **What keeps you up at night about the work you do?** What energizes you in the morning?

### Business & Operations (15 minutes)

8. **What are the top 3 reasons families choose RCL over other sober living homes?** (Based on actual conversations)

9. **What objections or concerns do you hear most during intake calls?** How do you typically address them?

10. **Walk me through your ideal intake process.** If you had a magic wand, what would the experience be like?

11. **How do you currently track success with residents?** What outcomes matter most to you?

12. **What percentage of inquiries convert to residents?** What typically prevents conversion?

13. **Where do your best referrals come from?** Treatment centers, families, word of mouth?

14. **What would make your life easier in managing inquiries and onboarding?** What takes too much time right now?

### Website Vision (15 minutes)

15. **When someone lands on the new website, what feeling should they have in the first 5 seconds?**

16. **What story about RCL needs to be told that isn't being told anywhere right now?**

17. **If you could show families one thing to build trust immediately, what would it be?** (video, photo, statistic, testimonial?)

18. **What questions do families NOT ask but should?** What do you wish they knew upfront?

19. **How do you want families to feel after exploring the website compared to your competitors?**

20. **Are you willing to be featured prominently on the website?** (video, photos, written story?) What feels comfortable?

### White-Label Platform Vision (10 minutes)

21. **What do other sober living providers struggle with that RCL has figured out?**

22. **If you were selling this as a platform, what would be your top 3 selling points?**

23. **What advice would you give someone starting a sober living home today?** What do they need on day one?

## Interview Synthesis Process

### After Each Interview:

1. **Immediate Documentation** (Within 2 hours)
   - Type up raw notes
   - Highlight direct quotes (use for website copy)
   - Note emotional moments or passion points
   - Flag any surprising insights

2. **Theme Identification** (Within 24 hours)
   - What patterns are emerging across interviews?
   - What pain points are mentioned repeatedly?
   - What language do they use to describe RCL?
   - What fears or objections come up?

3. **Content Extraction** (Within 48 hours)
   - Pull testimonial-worthy quotes
   - Identify story angles for website
   - Note missing information we need to address
   - Flag features that would solve mentioned problems

### Cross-Interview Analysis:

**Compare Perspectives:**
- How do Slade/Chloe's vision align with resident experience?
- Do family concerns match what house managers address?
- Are referral partner criteria being met in resident outcomes?

**Messaging Alignment:**
- What words do multiple stakeholders use? (Use this language)
- What values are universally expressed? (Core messaging)
- What differentiators are mentioned organically? (Competitive advantages)

**Feature Validation:**
- Which planned website features solve real pain points?
- What unexpected needs emerged that we should add?
- What assumptions were wrong and need revision?

## Interview Ethics & Permissions

### Confidentiality:
- **Resident Privacy:** Always get written permission for any identifiable content
- **Anonymous Options:** Offer first-name-only or completely anonymous participation
- **Family Sensitivity:** Respect that not all stories should be public
- **Professional Boundaries:** Don't share one stakeholder's comments with others

### Consent Forms Needed:
- [ ] **Photo/Video Release** (for anyone featured on website)
- [ ] **Testimonial Release** (for using quotes/stories)
- [ ] **Interview Recording Permission** (if recording conversations)
- [ ] **Minor Consent** (if any family members are under 18)

### Trauma-Informed Approach:
- **Avoid triggering questions** about specific trauma details
- **Give control** - let them decide what to share
- **Provide resources** if interview brings up difficult emotions
- **Follow up** to ensure they feel okay after interview
