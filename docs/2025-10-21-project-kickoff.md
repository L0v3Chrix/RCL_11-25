# Project Kickoff - Recovery Centered Living Website Redesign

**Date:** October 21, 2025
**Client:** Recovery Centered Living (RCL)
**Founders:** Slade & Chloe Skaggs
**Project Type:** Complete website redesign + white-label platform development
**Location:** Austin, Texas

---

## Context

Recovery Centered Living is a network of **six peer-led sober living homes** in Austin, Texas, founded by Slade and Chloe Skaggs. The organization embodies compassion, accountability, and authenticity rooted in lived experience and deep empathy for those navigating recovery.

This project represents a **dual opportunity:**

1. **Flagship Site:** Create an exceptional, conversion-focused website for RCL
2. **Platform Product:** Build a modular, white-label solution to resell to other sober living providers nationwide

---

## Why This Project Exists

### Current State Limitations:
- Existing website doesn't capture the warmth and spirit of RCL's leadership
- No interactive showcase of all six houses with real-time availability
- Missing digital intake and virtual tour capabilities
- Limited ability to demonstrate outcomes and success stories
- No member/family portal for ongoing engagement

### Vision for New Site:
- **Authentic storytelling** that reflects Slade and Chloe's journey and RCL's soul
- **Evidence-based positioning** showing real outcomes and success rates
- **Seamless digital experience** from discovery to intake to residency
- **Family engagement tools** for transparency and trust-building
- **Scalable architecture** enabling white-label deployment for other providers

## Design Principles

### Emotional Tone:
- **Warm, inviting, empowering** (not clinical or cold)
- **Hopeful and grounded** (inspire without overpromising)
- **Authentic and real** (lived experience, not stock imagery)
- **Spiritually connected** (without being dogmatic)
- **Community-centered** (mutual support, not institutional)

### Visual Direction:
- **Photography:** Real people, real houses, real moments (no stock photos)
- **Color Palette:** Warm, earthy, calming (avoid harsh clinical colors)
- **Typography:** Approachable yet professional (balance warmth with credibility)
- **Layout:** Breathing room, generous white space, uncluttered
- **Interactions:** Gentle, purposeful micro-animations (not flashy)

### User Experience:
- **Low-friction intake:** 3-step process (prescreen → full app → interview scheduling)
- **Quick access to critical info:** "Need Help Now?" prominent placement
- **Story-driven navigation:** Lead with transformation, not features
- **Mobile-first design:** Accessible during crisis moments on any device
- **Inclusive language:** Person-first, non-stigmatizing, empowering

## Technical Requirements

### Must-Haves:
- Next.js 14-15 with App Router and TypeScript
- Headless CMS (Sanity or Contentful) for content flexibility
- Mobile-first responsive design
- Accessibility compliance (WCAG 2.1 AA minimum)
- HIPAA-compliant data handling (if storing health information)
- Fast page speed (Lighthouse 90+ scores)
- SEO optimization for local Austin search

### Integrations Needed:
- SMS/text notifications for quick response
- Email automation for intake and follow-up
- Payment processing (Stripe for deposits/rent)
- Calendar/scheduling for tours and interviews
- CRM connection (if existing system, otherwise build simple one)
- Analytics (PostHog or GA4)

### Future Platform Features:
- White-label theming (logo, colors, content swap)
- Multi-tenant architecture (multiple providers in one system)
- Admin configuration UI (drag-and-drop content builder)
- Resource library manager (city/state-specific recovery resources)
- Reporting dashboard (outcomes, occupancy, referrals)
